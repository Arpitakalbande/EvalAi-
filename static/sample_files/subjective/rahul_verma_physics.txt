Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton made three laws of motion. The first law says things don't move unless you push them. The second law is F = ma which means heavier things need more force. Third law is action reaction which means equal and opposite forces.

Momentum is conserved when no force acts. Rockets use this.

Friction is the force that stops things from moving. There is static and kinetic friction.

Circular motion has a force toward center called centripetal. Roads are sometimes tilted at curves.
