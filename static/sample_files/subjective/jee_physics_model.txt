NEWTON'S LAWS OF MOTION — COMPLETE NOTES

Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia):
A body continues to remain in its state of rest or uniform motion along a straight line unless compelled by an external net force to change that state. Inertia is the intrinsic property of matter that resists any change in its state of motion.

Newton's Second Law:
The net external force acting on a body is equal to the rate of change of its linear momentum.
F = dp/dt = ma (when mass is constant)
where p = mv is the linear momentum. The direction of acceleration is always the same as the net applied force.

Newton's Third Law:
Every action has an equal and opposite reaction. Forces always occur in action-reaction pairs, acting on different bodies simultaneously. They are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction.

Impulse-Momentum Theorem:
Impulse = Force x time = J = F.dt = change in momentum = m(v - u)
Important in collisions and explosions where large forces act for very short time intervals.

Law of Conservation of Linear Momentum:
If the net external force on a system is zero, the total linear momentum of the system remains constant.
m1u1 + m2u2 = m1v1 + m2v2 (two-body system)
This is applicable in explosions, collisions, and rocket propulsion.

Types of Collisions:
1. Elastic Collision: Both kinetic energy and momentum are conserved. e = 1 (coefficient of restitution).
2. Inelastic Collision: Only momentum is conserved; kinetic energy decreases. e < 1.
3. Perfectly Inelastic: Bodies stick together after collision. Maximum loss of kinetic energy. e = 0.

Friction:
Static friction: fs <= us x N; prevents relative motion between surfaces.
Kinetic friction: fk = uk x N; opposes relative sliding motion.
Always us > uk. Friction is independent of contact area. Depends only on nature of surfaces and normal reaction.

Laws of Friction: Friction is proportional to normal force, independent of area, depends on nature of surfaces.

Circular Motion and Centripetal Force:
Centripetal acceleration: a = v^2/r = omega^2 x r (directed toward center)
Centripetal force: F = mv^2/r (must be provided by a real force — tension, friction, normal force, gravity)
Banking angle: tan(theta) = v^2/rg for frictionless banked road.

Applications:
- Rocket propulsion uses Newton's Third Law (gas expelled back, rocket goes forward)
- Walking: push ground backward, ground pushes you forward
- Recoil of gun: momentum conserved; bullet goes forward, gun recoils backward
- Banking of roads: resolves need for friction in curved paths
